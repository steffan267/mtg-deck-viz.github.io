## Narrow implementation partition

G017 should not be coded as one monolithic “lock detector”. The current corpus
supports four auditable subfamilies plus one explicit residual set.

### 1. Forced-cast prohibition locks

Target rows:

- `Knowledge Pool` / `Omen Machine` plus zone/timing/payment cast restrictions.

Proposed generic capabilities:

- `is-forced-nonhand-cast-engine`
  - text patterns like “cast it without paying its mana cost if able” or
    “may cast ... from among exiled/revealed cards”
  - must be forced off a non-hand zone or draw-replacement path
- `forced-cast-origin:exile|library-top`
- `forced-cast-payment:free`
- `is-cast-origin-lockpiece`
  - examples of semantics, not names:
    - opponents can’t cast from exile / graveyard / non-hand zones
    - players can’t cast more than one spell each turn
    - opponents can cast spells only as sorceries
    - spells cast without mana spent are countered
    - spells cast with no colored mana spent are countered

Proposed strict families:

- `forced-exile-cast→origin-lock`
- `forced-draw-replacement-cast→origin-lock`

Proof contract:

1. the engine forces or offers the replacement cast from a zone other than hand;
2. the paired lockpiece forbids or nullifies that cast on the same axis
   (zone, timing, spell-count, or free-cast payment profile);
3. the original spell/card is already consumed/exiled/replaced before the
   forbidden follow-up cast would happen;
4. the family claims `lock` only.

This slice is high-value and low-risk because the rows are currently all
`missed` and the semantics are text-local.

### 2. Counter-suppression prevention / depletion locks

Target rows:

- `Solemnity + Nine Lives`
- `Solemnity + Decree of Silence`
- `Solemnity + Delaying Shield`
- `Solemnity + Glacial Chasm`
- `Solemnity + Phyrexian Unlife`

Proposed generic capabilities:

- `is-counter-suppression-static`
  - “players can’t get counters”
  - “counters can’t be put on artifacts, creatures, enchantments, or lands”
- `is-damage-prevention-counter-burden`
  - damage to you is prevented/replaced by putting counters on the same
    permanent, and the lock fails or flips once counters accumulate
- `is-depletion-counter-lockpiece`
  - counters on the same permanent consume a lock/counterspell effect and
    sacrifice or disable it at a threshold
- `is-poison-loss-replacement-shield`
  - damage to you below 0 life is converted into poison counters

Proposed strict families:

- `counter-suppression→prevention-lock`
- `counter-suppression→depletion-counter-lock`
- `counter-suppression→poison-loss-lock`

Proof contract:

1. the prevention or counterspell shield depends on counters being added;
2. the suppression effect forbids those counters on the relevant object/player;
3. failure text that depends on the counters therefore never advances;
4. result claims remain `lock` only.

Important guardrail:

- do **not** infer arbitrary replacement-layer interactions;
- require direct card-text linkage between the prevented counters and the
  maintained shield/failure condition.

### 3. Replay-maintained prevention locks

Target rows:

- `Glacial Chasm` plus extra-land-play / graveyard-land replay support.
- Some One Ring / protection rows may partially overlap if the protection
  source is re-established each turn via blink/recast, but that should be kept
  separate until proven safe.

Proposed generic capabilities:

- `is-full-self-damage-prevention-source`
  - “prevent all damage that would be dealt to you”
- `is-self-burdened-lock-land`
  - land/permanent supplies the shield but self-sacrifices or is hard to keep
    without repeating its entry / replay
- `is-extra-land-play-enabler`
- `is-land-yard-replay-enabler`

Proposed strict family:

- `land-replay→damage-prevention-lock`

Proof contract:

1. one piece grants the full self-damage prevention shield;
2. one piece replays lands from graveyard or restores the land body each turn;
3. one piece increases land-play capacity when replaying is required on the
   same turn cycle;
4. the maintained state proves only `lock`.

Guardrails:

- no ambient “you can probably pay the upkeep” assumptions;
- no generic life-total or land-count assumptions beyond the explicit replay
  loop state named in the proof;
- if the shield depends on extra unsupported resource production, defer it.

### 4. Untap-step / next-turn control locks

This needs two separate proof surfaces:

#### 4a. Global or opponent untap-step denial

Target rows:

- `Stasis` families
- `Brine Elemental + Vesuvan Shapeshifter`

Proposed generic capabilities:

- `is-global-untap-skipper`
- `is-opponent-next-untap-skipper`
- `is-self-untap-breaker`
  - untap your permanents during your turns despite the skip shell
- `is-repeatable-face-reset-engine`
  - for turn-face-down / turn-face-up loops that re-fire the untap denial

Potential strict families:

- `global-untap-skip→self-breaker-lock`
- `repeatable-next-untap-skip→turn-lock`

#### 4b. Opponent-turn control locks

Target rows:

- `Mindslaver` plus replay/access engines.

Proposed generic capabilities:

- `is-opponent-turn-control-activation`
- `is-artifact-topdeck-recursion`
- `is-repeatable-artifact-yard-cast`
- `is-repeatable-artifact-copy-as-sacrificed`

Potential strict family:

- `turn-control-replay→lock`

Proof burden is much higher here:

1. the turn-control effect must be repeatable on successive opponent turns;
2. the package must recover the same control artifact after sacrifice;
3. mana / draw-access continuity must be explicit or package-local.

This is likely the last G017 coding slice, not the first.

## Recommended implementation order

1. **Forced-cast prohibition locks**
   - best combination of row count, clarity, and text-local proofability.
2. **Counter-suppression prevention/depletion locks**
   - highest volume after that; still text-local if kept narrow.
3. **Replay-maintained prevention locks**
   - especially Glacial Chasm-style land replay.
4. **Untap-step denial locks**
   - split `Stasis` and `Brine` semantics if needed.
5. **Opponent-turn control locks**
   - only after the earlier slices land safely.

## Safety contract for G017

Non-negotiable constraints:

- no runtime or evaluator card-name matching;
- no broad `EDGE_RESULT_CLASS_MAP` inflation from lock keywords alone;
- result claims stay conservative (`lock` only unless a proof package already
  proves another axis independently);
- every family needs positive, negative, and evaluator regression tests;
- residual unsafe cases must be recorded for human review rather than counted.

## Immediate next coding target

Start with **forced-cast prohibition locks**:

- add classifier capabilities for non-hand forced-cast engines and cast-origin /
  timing / free-cast restrictions;
- add one or two proof-only families covering the Knowledge Pool / Omen
  Machine rows;
- seed proof packages conservatively;
- validate against representative rows before broadening into the Solemnity /
  Glacial / Stasis partitions.

This landed as a new strict proof/package family:

- `global-untap-skip→self-bounce-lock`

The proof is intentionally narrow:

- the support must be a repeatable self-bounce effect;
- it must be usable outside your own turn, so the untap-skip piece can leave
  the battlefield before your next untap step;
- it must be able to target the lockpiece you control;
- the proof records any extra discard, creature-tap, Bird-tap, snow-count, or
  mana-reservation assumptions rather than hiding them.

The intentionally deferred strict residuals in this mini-cluster are the
remaining `Stasis` shells that need a different semantic surface:

- upkeep-funding lines such as `Stasis + Smothering Tithe`
- attack-trigger or combat-refresh lines such as `Jorn, God of Winter + Stasis`
- tutor/tuck reset shells such as `Zur the Enchanter + Stasis + Tel-Jilad Stylus`
- copy/replay shells such as `Trade Caravan + Stasis + Mirrormade`

Those stay out of this family because they are not plain “bounce before untap,
then replay” loops.
