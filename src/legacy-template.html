MTG Deck Interaction Map

This shows how the cards in a Commander deck actually work together — not just what they are.

What the dots & lines mean

Each dot is a card, colored by its job (ramp, draw, removal…). A line means two cards mechanically interact — one does something the other cares about. Line strength shows how real the synergy is: faint grey = incidental, blue = moderate, green = strong, orange = combo-critical.

Position shows power. Each card has a mass that pulls it toward the centre, so the deck's most powerful and most-connected cards sit in the core and chaff drifts to the rim. The gold-ringed card in the middle is your commander (or, with no commander, the heaviest card). Bigger dot = heavier.

Three score axes

Win tuning (0–100) rates how directly the list is optimized to win: speed, tutors, card flow, answers, compact finishers, resilience, efficiency, Game Changers and deck size. A one-line plain-English summary tells you how the deck wins, and hovering each bar lists the cards behind it. Cohesion rates synergy — winning through an engine of cards that feed each other. Self-sufficiency rates standalone card strength.

Game Changers are WotC's official Commander-Brackets power cards. The bracket classifier also checks the Beta rules for mass land denial, chained extra turns, two-card combo timing, tutor density, and tuning. A combat deck can be cohesive and resilient without being faster than a tutor/wheel/finisher deck. Read the three scores together. ⚡ flags a detected combo.

Add & compare decks

Click “+ Add Moxfield deck” (top-left) and paste a Moxfield deck link — e.g. moxfield.com/decks/…. It loads instantly.

Add as many as you like, switch between them in the sidebar, and click “compare ▸” to see their numbers side by side. (Adding decks needs an internet connection.)

Tips

Drag dots around · scroll to zoom · drag the background to pan. Use Hide isolated to drop cards that don’t interact. The Gravity button switches what pulls cards inward: links + power (both), links · size = power (position = synergy, size = standalone power), or power-weighted links (linking to strong cards pulls hardest — the true engine knots up).
